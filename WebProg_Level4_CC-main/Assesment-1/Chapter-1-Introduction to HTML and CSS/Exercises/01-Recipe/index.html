BROOKIES

Giving you the best of both worlds!

Chocolate Chip Cookie Brownies, shortened to 'Brookies', should be a staple in every household, a sweet treat no one can resist! A hybrid of these two tasty snacks bring life into any occasion, combining such classic pastries into one singular dessert, packing the smell of "home" wherever you go.

I'm sure that many among us back in the day have smelled the sweet alluring scent of freshly baked cookies or brownies, before digging into the most wonderful, deliciously sweet goods ever known to man. Whether or not you've experienced this, this recipe will rekindle that delightful nostalgia, making you definitely crave for more.

Ingredients to make Brookies

Cookie Layer

  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • ½ cup light brown sugar
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

Brownie Layer

  • 1 cup white sugar
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 eggs
  • ⅓ cup cocoa powder
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon baking powder
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt

How to Make Brookies

Preparation

  1. Preheat the oven at 350° Farenheit for around 30-40 minutes.
  2. Spray a 9 x 13 baking tin with non-stick, overlining it with parchment paper leaving an overhang. Spray it once more then set aside.

Cookie Dough

  1. In a medium sized bowl, whisk the flour, salt, and baking soda together, then set aside.
  2. Next, beat the softened butter, light brown sugar, and white sugar in a large metal bowl on medium-high.
  3. Once beat, add in the egg and vanilla extract into the mixture.
  4. Sift in the flour mixture, incorporating it slowly but surely on low-speed.
  5. Lastly, gently stir in the semi-sweet chocolate chips evenly across the dough to finalize the first part of this recipe.

Brownie Batter

  1. In a separate bowl, mix in the sugar and vanilla extract into the melted butter.
  2. When done mixing, incorporate each egg one at a time.
  3. Next, whisk in the cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt
  4. To finish the brownie batter, ensure the all-purpose flour is combined well after folding.

Final Stage

  1. Using the baking tin from the preparation stage, pour the brownie batter inside to create the first layer.
  2. Then, scoop up the cookie dough into small portions, placing each piece evenly across the brownie batter to allow spreading.
  3. Cover the tin lightly with a layer of foil then bake for 20 minutes.
  4. Next, remove the foil then bake once more for another 30 minutes.
  5. Lastly, cool the product for approximately 20 minutes. Once done, cut into squares then it's all ready to serve!

Serve Well and Enjoy!<3
